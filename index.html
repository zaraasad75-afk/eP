The Tile Guide

Your complete ready reference before buying — types, sizes, finishes & more

Most Popular
Ceramic Tiles

Made from fired clay. Affordable & easy to cut. Best for indoor walls, bedrooms & low-traffic areas.

Premium
Porcelain Tiles

Denser than ceramic with very low water absorption. Perfect for bathrooms, kitchens & outdoor areas.

High Traffic
Vitrified Tiles

Extremely hard & non-porous. Ideal for high-traffic floors in living rooms & commercial spaces.

Luxury
Marble / Natural Stone

Cut from natural stone — every tile is unique. Stunning look for living rooms & feature walls.

Decorative
Mosaic Tiles

Small tiles in patterns — glass, ceramic or stone. Great for feature walls, shower niches & backsplashes.

Speciality
Special Function Tiles

Germ Free, Anti-Static, TAC Tactile & Cool Roof — engineered for hygiene, safety & heat resistance.

💡 Pro Tip: For bathrooms and kitchens, always choose porcelain or vitrified over ceramic — they absorb less water and last much longer in wet areas.

🏠 Quick Rule: Wet areas always need waterproof + anti-skid tiles. Dry areas like bedrooms and living rooms — focus on look and feel.
1
Daily Cleaning

Sweep or vacuum to remove dust and grit. Damp mop with mild detergent. Avoid harsh scrubbing on polished tiles — it dulls the finish.

2
Grout Care

Clean grout lines with a soft brush and baking soda paste. Re-seal grout every 1–2 years in wet areas like bathrooms and kitchens.

3
Marble & Natural Stone

Seal every 6–12 months to prevent staining. Never use acidic cleaners like vinegar or lemon — they permanently etch the surface.

4
What to Avoid

No bleach on colored grout. No steel wool or abrasive pads. Don't drag heavy furniture on polished tiles — always use felt pads.

5
Stain Removal

Oil stains → dish soap + warm water. Rust → oxalic acid cleaner. Soap scum → diluted white vinegar works well on ceramic and porcelain.

6
Tile Lifespan

Ceramic: 10–20 yrs · Porcelain: 20–50 yrs · Vitrified: 25+ yrs · Natural Stone: 50–100 yrs with proper care and maintenance.

🛠️ Installation Tip: Always buy 10–15% extra tiles beyond your room measurement to account for cuts, breakage & future replacements.
